Air Quality Index Scale

  • 0–50: Good – Minimal impact on health.
  • 51–100: Moderate – Acceptable, but some pollutants may affect sensitive groups.
  • 101–200: Poor – May cause breathing discomfort, especially for children and elderly.
  • 201+: Very Poor / Hazardous – Serious health risks for everyone.

Types of Harmful Air Pollutants

PM2.5

Tiny particles less than 2.5 microns.

PM10

Dust and smoke particles up to 10 microns.

NOx

Produced by vehicles and industry.

SO₂

From burning coal and oil.

CO

Colorless, odorless gas from combustion.

O₃

Ground-level ozone forms smog.
